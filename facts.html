Did You Know?

The "Snipe" Original Vision

Before they were Soul Reapers, they were Snipers. Tite Kubo’s first draft featured
characters using firearms instead of katanas. He eventually realized that swords allowed for more personal, high-stakes
combat, leading to the creation of the Zanpakuto.

Early Rejection

Early on, Bleach was rejected by the editors at Weekly Shonen Jump. Kubo was ready to give up until he received a
personal letter from Akira Toriyama, the creator of Dragon Ball. Toriyama loved the art style and encouraged Kubo to keep
going. Without the father of Dragon Ball, Bleach might never have existed!

Linguistic World-Building

Tite Kubo made use of linguistic world-building to give each side in Bleach a unique cultural "flavor," which fans
can immediately recognize a character's affiliation to based on their use of terms. The Soul Reapers are based on Japanese
culture, represented by words like Bankai and Shikai that reflect the theme of spirituality and tradition. The Arrancars have
a Spanish theme, as represented by locations like Hueco Mundo (Hollow World) and Las Noches (The Nights). The Quincies have
a German theme in terms of their hierarchy and abilities, like Sternritter (Star Knights) and Heilig Pfeil. The Fullbringers
have an English theme in terms of their locations and abilities, like Book of the End and Dirty Boots.

Fashion-Forward Mangaka

Tite Kubo is the most "fashion-forward" mangaka in the industry. He has stated that if he weren't an artist, he would have
pursued fashion design. This is why Bleach characters often appear in trendy streetwear in official art spreads—Kubo simply loves
drawing cool clothes!

The Espada

The Espada (the top ten villains) aren't just muscle; they each represent a specific human "reason for death," adding
philosophical weight to their fights:

Starrk (1st): Loneliness

Barragan (2nd): Time/Old Age

Ulquiorra (4th): Emptiness/Nihilism

Grimmjow (6th): Destruction

Long Wait for the Final Arc

After the original anime was canceled in 2012, fans waited 10 years for the final arc, the Thousand-Year Blood War, to be
animated. It finally returned in 2022 with movie-quality animation, proving the series' legacy is immortal.

Kubo's Musical Influences

Kubo is a massive fan of Rock and Punk music. He actually curated an official "Character Theme" playlist to help him get into
the right mindset while drawing:

Ichigo Kurosaki: "News from the Front" by Bad Religion

Renji Abarai: "Nora Inu" by Hazu

Kenpachi Zaraki: "Superbeast" by Rob Zombie

Ichigo's Name is a Wordplay

Ichigo’s name is a classic Japanese wordplay.The Numbers: "Ichi" means $1$ and "Go" means $5$. This is why the number 15
appears constantly in his room and on his clothing.The Fruit: "Ichigo" is also the Japanese word for Strawberry, a frequent
joke regarding his bright orange hair.
